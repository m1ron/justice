Think of the justice system as a pipeline

In broken systems the poor are not protected.

The exploited remain vulnerable and victims routinely experience further trauma by attempting to seek justice.

IJM works within these fractured systems, crawling through the pipeline ourselves pushing through case after case. We identify leaks, clogs and disconnects. We then work collaboratively and systematically with stakeholders to strengthen the entire system so that it is sustainable and effective.

Cybersex trafficking

A new systemic threat to children

Cybersex trafficking involves the production and online publication of video or images of the sexual abuse or exploitation of a minor for a paying third party who is not in the physical presence of the victim.

In the Philippines it is a pervasive, unaddressed and growing crime.
